1942-1945

Bracero Experiences

The Bracero Program is seldom taught in American History courses and the experiences of the individuals involved even less so. Below are some of the experiences that Braceros went through here in the United States.

Bracero Archives Defiant Bracero

Woman Experiences

Braceros were men which meant husbands, fathers, sons, and brothers would head north across the border leaving behind wives, mothers, daughters, and sisters. Some women waited for their return. Others journeyed north to be with them.

Men Experiences

Although the program was praised as a noble way of making more money while also contributing to the war effort, some men would spend their money in the U.S in devious vices. Some of these included drinking, prostitution, gambling, and other vices.

Unseen: Homosexuality

Another, often untalked, aspect of the program was homosexuality. There's evidence that it happened in the camps. Prostitution was present in the camps, yet some men participated in sexual relationships with other men. Other men didn't seem to condemn this.

Unseen: Homosexuality (continued)

They were more concerned with being inconvenienced by it. For example, a lover would cut the lights so no one would discover the identity of his lover. The men didn't confront the man based on his sexual preference but their annoyance of the light being cut.

Bracero Families

Sometimes Braceros would leave families behind in Mexico and make new ones in the U.S. Sometimes they stayed with thes new families and other times they returned home and abandoned their U.S families.

Bracero Families (continued)

This became such a problem that some Mexican women appealed to the Mexican government to force their husbands to support them financially or return home. Others, emboldened by the absence of their husband, simply sought out new partners.
